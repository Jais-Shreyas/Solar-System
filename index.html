Our Solar-System

(Hold left-click on a planet to know more.)

MERCURY

Distance from Sun: 66.78 million km.
1 year on Mercury = 87.97 Earth-days.
1st planet from the Sun, smallest in the Solar System.
Named after ancient Roman God Mercurius.

VENUS

Distance from Sun: 107.49 million km.
1 year on Venus = 224.70 Earth-days.
Has the densest atmosphere of the terrestrial planets.
Mean temperature: 464 deg. C | Pressure : 92 atm.

EARTH

Distance from Sun: 152.06 million km.
1 year on Earth = 365.26 Earth-days.
Only planet with life on it.
Has a single moon: the Moon.

MARS

Distance from Sun: 212.65 million km.
1 year on Mars = 686.98 Earth-days.
Also called Red Planet because of its reddish appearance.
Contains water and dry ice on polar ice caps.

JUPITER

Distance from Sun: 778.3 million km.
1 year on Jupiter = 11.86 Earth-years.
Largest planet in the Solar system.
Third brightest object in the Earth's night sky after the Moon and Venus.
Has around 95 moons officially recognised.

SATURN

Distance from Sun: 1,447.2 million km.
1 year on Saturn = 29.46 Earth-years.
Second largest planet in the Solar system after Jupiter.
Has around 146 moons officially recognised.
Wind speed can reach upto 1,800 km/h.

URANUS

Distance from Sun: 2,928.2 million km.
1 year on Uranus = 84.01 Earth-years.
Gaseous cyan-coloured ice giant.
Notable axial-tilt of 82.23 deg. causing 42 years of day and night cycles each.

NEPTUNE

Distance from Sun: 4,471.9 million km.
1 year on Neptune = 164.79 Earth-years.
Eighth and farthest known planet in the Solar System.
Only planet that was found by mathematical predictions.
Not visible to unaided eye.
